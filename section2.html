It is important to understand different types of threats you may face so you can overcome them.

Cyber Attacks

Malware

Malware

Malware, short for "malicious software," refers to any software specifically designed to cause harm to a computer system, network, or device. Malware can take various forms and can be programmed to execute a wide range of destructive or disruptive activities.

×

Phishing Email

Phishing Email

A phishing email is a type of cyber attack where the attacker poses as a legitimate entity or organization in an attempt to deceive the recipient into providing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, financial information, or personal details. These emails often appear to come from reputable sources, such as banks, social media platforms, online retailers, or government agencies, and typically employ tactics to create a sense of urgency or fear to prompt the recipient to take immediate action.

×

Password Cracking

Password Cracking

A password cracking attack is a cybersecurity technique used to gain unauthorized access to a system or an account by systematically attempting to guess or decipher passwords. This type of attack exploits weaknesses in password security, such as using weak passwords, predictable patterns, or insufficiently protected authentication mechanisms.

×

Man in the Middle attack

Man in the Middle attack

A man-in-the-middle (MITM) attack is a type of cyber attack where a malicious actor intercepts and potentially alters communication between two parties who believe they are directly communicating with each other. The attacker secretly relays and possibly modifies the communication between the two parties, allowing them to eavesdrop on the conversation, steal sensitive information, or manipulate the data exchanged between the legitimate parties.

×

Denial-of-Sevice

Denial-of-Sevice

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal functioning of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming it with a flood of illegitimate traffic. The aim of such an attack is to make the targeted resource unavailable to its intended users, effectively denying them access to the service.

×

Spoofing

Spoofing

A spoofing attack is a type of cyber attack where a malicious actor impersonates another entity or device on a network in order to deceive or manipulate users, systems, or services. This impersonation is typically achieved by falsifying data such as IP addresses, email addresses, or other identifying information to appear as though it is originating from a trusted or legitimate source.
