Response

When an Attack happens, it is nesesary to have a plan in place so you know how to respond to mitgate as much damage as possible.

Response Plan

A response plan to a cyber attack is a structured strategy designed to guide users on what to do in event of a cybersecurity breach or incident.

Containment

The goal of containment is to limit damage from the current security incident and prevent any further damage.

  • Disconnect the affected network from the internet.
  • Disable all remote access to the network.
  • Re-route network traffic.
  • Change all vulnerable passwords.
×

Eradication

Eradication is intended to actually remove malware or other artifacts introduced by the attacks, and fully restore all affected systems.

  • complete wipe and re-image of affected system hard drives to ensure any malicious content is removed.
  • understanding what caused the incident preventing future compromise, for example by patching a vulnerability exploited by the attacker.
  • upgrading old software versions and disabling unused services.
  • use anti-malware software, or Next-Generation Antivirus (NGAV) if available, to scan affected systems and ensure all malicious content is removed.
×

Recovery

  • testing and verifying the system to ensure that it is clean and fully functional.
  • ongoing monitoring for some time after the incident to observe operations and check for abnormal behaviors.
  • considering what can be done on the restored systems to protect them from recurrence of the same incident.
